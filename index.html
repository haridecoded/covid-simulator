How Social Distancing Can Help Your Neighborhood Effectively Fight Coronavirus?

On December 26, 2019 four unusual cases of pneumonia was noticed by Dr. Jixian Xhang. On January 7th, Coronavirus (COVID-19) was identified as the cause. Since then, the virus has infected thousands of people all over the world at an exponential rate. 400,000+ cases so far!

To help you understand what is going on, let us think of a small neighborhood of 200 people. Can you guess how coronavirus will spread in this neighborhood in the next 30 days?

Draw your guesses on the chart below.

That is nearly of the neighborhood in 30 days!

An urgent problem is that hospitals are unable to manage this rapid and exponential growth of coronavirus cases. For every 10 people in the neighborhood, there is 1 hospital bed avaiable for care. This means, not everyone who is infected can get the treatment they need!

You can help slow down the rate at which coronavirus is spreading. The goal is to ensure that the number of patients are always fewer than the number of hospital beds available.

But first, let us tell you two key facts about how the virus spreads:

(1) The virus spreads from person-to-person when people are in close contact with one another (within 6 feet).
(2) A person may get coronavirus by touching a surface or object that has the virus on it and then touching their own face.
